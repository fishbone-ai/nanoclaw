# NetCost Pitch — Q&A Prep | 2026-04-17

> Keep this open during the call. Don't volunteer any of these — only use when asked.

| # | Question | Answer |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **What if ChatGPT changes the rules?** | Apps replaced instant checkout — OpenAI doubled down on this model in March 2026 after having both instant checkout and apps. |
| 2 | **Why not just go through Instacart?** | With Instacart, you're inside their ecosystem — their brand, their app, their customer relationship. The shopper connects to Instacart, not to NetCost. With your own app, the customer finds NetCost, connects to your account, browses your catalog, checks out on your site. The loyalty, the data, the relationship stays with you. It's the difference between having your own storefront and renting a shelf inside someone else's store. |
| 3 | **How many customers will realistically use this?** | Honest answer — the channel is new, no one has perfect data yet. What we know: 84M shopping questions per week on ChatGPT, 69% AI market share in the US, and the big players are betting on this channel and progressively joining. |
| 4 | **What data do you get access to?** | Checkout happens on your site. Customer data stays with you. We get structured search queries and cart interactions — not PII. |
| 5 | **What's the actual price?** | We're pricing this as a one-time setup fee plus a monthly retainer. The exact numbers — I'd rather put those on the table after we've scoped what a first launch looks like together, so the number actually reflects what we're building for you. That's what the next session would be for. |
| 6 | **Who else are you working with?** | Grocery is our vertical — it's where this shift is happening fastest. NetCost is our priority in the US right now because of the existing relationship with Stor.ai and because your catalog and customer base are a strong fit for this kind of experience. |
| 7 | **How do we measure ROI?** | Every order that starts in the ChatGPT app and completes on your site is fully attributable — direct channel revenue, easy to measure. Beyond that, conversational shopping naturally drives larger baskets and upsells — a customer asks for pasta ingredients, the app suggests the olive oil, the parmesan, the wine. That doesn't happen in traditional search-and-browse. In terms of breakeven — at an average basket of $80, even a few orders a day through the channel covers the retainer. Everything above that is pure upside. |
| 8 | **What about compliance / allergens in AI responses?** | The app surfaces your catalog data — descriptions, ingredients, images. We don't generate product information, we surface what's already in your system. |
| 9 | **What if a competitor launches their app first?** | Then you'll wish you started today. The first apps in a category owns the user habit — when someone connects ChatGPT for groceries app, the first app they connect to becomes their default. |
| 10 | **We don't have bandwidth for a new project.** | On your side it's coordination, not development. Zero new integrations — we build on top of Stor.ai. Fishbone and Stor.ai handle the build and the maintenance. |
| 11 | **Who maintains this after launch?** | We do. That's what the retainer covers — OpenAI platform updates, seasonal changes, catalog shifts, all on us. You're not hiring a team for this. |
