## 2026-04-21 — Ad Sync: Meta campaign launch + Knots experiment invalidation + Google Business Agent

**Source:** Call — [Ad Sync transcript](../calls/meetings/2026-04-21-ad-sync-2026-04-21-18-36-idt-recording.md)
**What we learned:** (1) Meta's within-ad-set algorithm dynamically shifts budget to the winning creative after ~2–3 days, making formal A/B testing unnecessary at small budgets; the threshold for statistically meaningful A/B tests is ~₪3k+ per variant. (2) The Knots GMC experiment is likely invalidated because Neta's paid ad campaign stopped on April 1, removing the traffic baseline needed to isolate catalog-change effects — external client changes to ad spend can silently kill experiment validity. (3) Google Business Agent (early-access GMC feature) lets brands configure an AI assistant (tone, welcome message, conversation starters) — appears to be a low-effort setup that could be packaged as part of an AI-visibility offering for retailers. (4) Direct WhatsApp CTA from a Meta ad (bypassing the landing page) trades measurement fidelity for reduced funnel friction — worth A/B testing once baseline conversion data exists.
**Implications:** Experiment isolation protocol needed: when running GMC catalog experiments, we must confirm with the client that no other traffic or budget changes will occur during the measurement window. Google Business Agent worth exploring as an upsell service (FB-125). Meta campaign for "Better Review" is live — monitor at day 3 and day 5 (FB-197).

## 2026-04-15 -- B2C GTM: paid ads buy speed, organic trades time for money

**Source:** Ohav & Avishay sync (transcript: `calls/meetings/2026-04-15-ydd-kpjx-mrt-2026-04-15-14-41-gmt-3.md`)
**What we learned:** For a B2C consumer app with no paid ads expertise in-house, paid ads buy faster signal but require marketing know-how and upfront spend that may not be justified at early validation stage. Organic distribution (FB/WA groups) is slower but costs only time. Emotional/transformation-led messaging ("become the person who shows up consistently") likely outperforms performance-metric framing ("like having a personal trainer at a fraction of the cost"). Over-personalised or aggressive ads risk alienating users even if they're memorable. Need ~100 conversions for statistically meaningful signal; below that you're in noise. Separately: session context continuity matters -- each day the agent runs without persistent memory is organizational knowledge lost, and the gap between what's stored on the Pi vs. in the repo is a real blind spot.
**Implications:** Before spending on B2C ads, consult someone with hands-on Meta/Google ads experience. Prioritize fixing NanoClaude MCP connectivity to restore agent context continuity. For Store pitch: use transformation framing over raw stats where possible.

## 2026-04-17 — Annual vs monthly framing in early B2B SaaS deals

**Implications:** Formalize dual-tier pricing model. Build a scoping template for retainer deliverables before next deal -- vagueness that feels safe now becomes a liability as deals close. Supports FB-122 (what Fishbone actually delivers).

## 2026-04-16 — SaaS pricing for innovation-stage prospects: anchor high, tier by volume, minimize downside

**Source:** Call -- Morris quick call (2026-04-16), pre-pitch prep with advisor Morris (`calls/meetings/2026-04-16-morris-quick-call.md`)
**What we learned:** When selling to a prospect who treats Fishbone as an "innovation" experiment, a flat high monthly fee feels risky. Right structure: one-time setup anchored at 15-20k (negotiate to 10k floor) + tiered monthly starting low (~2.5k) scaling to 5k as the prospect's sales volume grows. Morris's principle: the prospect's cost of failure must feel low to get the deal done. Also confirmed -- Fishbone is SaaS; IP never transfers. Clients who leave go find another provider, they don't take code.
**Implications:** Need a formalized tiered pricing model before next prospect meetings. Supports FB-122 (Fishbone beats DIY/PIM) and FB-125 (retailers recognize gap). Volume-based tiers also create a natural upsell path as the prospect grows.

## 2026-04-16 — NanoClaw replaces OpenClaw

**Source:** platform migration
**What we learned:** OneCLI's credential injection doesn't match Claude Agent SDK's auth path (SDK uses `x-api-key`, OneCLI injects `Authorization: Bearer`). For a single-user self-hosted setup, NanoClaw's native credential proxy is strictly simpler and removes a whole layer of misbehavior.
**Implications:** Future Claude-based assistant work defaults to native credential proxy; keep OneCLI in mind only for multi-tenant / multi-secret deployments.
