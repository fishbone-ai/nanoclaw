Initiative Overview
AI Visibility Initiative

Shoppers increasingly start their search in AI assistants rather than traditional search engines. When someone asks "best heated brush for thick frizzy hair", AI systems surface products based on the quality and completeness of their catalog data - attributes like color, product type, hair type, structured descriptions, and brand consistency.

This initiative is about understanding what moves the needle for DAFNI Hair specifically, and doing it in a way we can measure clearly with a controlled test.

Baseline Assessment
Where Things Stand Today

We reviewed your full Google Merchant Center catalog. Here's what we found across the fields AI systems use to match products to queries:

Field Status
Color Populated on only 3 of 36 products — the field AI shopping uses most heavily for variant matching
Age group & gender Almost entirely unset (3 and 2 of 36) — limits how AI assistants filter results for shopper queries
Brand Inconsistent formatting across the catalog (mix of "DAFNI®" and "DAFNI EN") — weakens brand entity recognition
Product type Set on most products but inconsistent casing and granularity; not aligned to a clean taxonomy
Structured attributes Hair type, material, and product highlights not populated
The Work
What We're Doing

Rather than enriching the whole catalog at once, we're running a controlled matched-pair test. We've selected 4 pairs of products (8 products total) across Klix Singles, Bows, Chunky Bows, and Hair Care Kits. Within each pair, products are closely matched on historical impressions and sales — so any difference we observe afterward can be attributed to the enrichment, not to one product being intrinsically stronger.

For each test product, we're enriching the key attributes Google and AI assistants use for matching — color, product type, brand consistency, structured taxonomy fields, descriptions, and product highlights. The matched control product is left unchanged as the comparison baseline.

Changes are made directly in Shopify, which syncs to Google Merchant Center automatically. Prices, inventory, storefront, and campaign settings are untouched. If the test works, we roll the same playbook out to the rest of the catalog with confidence.
