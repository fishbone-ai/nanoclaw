# Catalog & Content Layer — Competitive Intelligence
**Date:** 2026-03-10

## Context: What Fishbone Does

Fishbone does AI-powered product catalog enrichment for e-commerce — taking raw/incomplete product data and enriching it with structured attributes, descriptions, categorization, and metadata to make it usable for search, AI agents, personalization, and marketplace distribution.

---

## Company Profiles

---

### 1. Hypotenuse AI

**What they do:**
AI-powered e-commerce catalog enrichment platform that generates product descriptions, fills missing attributes, standardizes data, and provides bulk image editing. Founded 2020, YC-backed, ~25 employees.

**Positioning:**
"The AI content platform for e-commerce teams" — marketed to teams managing high-volume catalogs who need SEO-optimized content at scale with PIM/Shopify integrations.

**Funding:**
Seed stage — YC graduate. Estimated ARR ~$2.8M as of early 2026.

**Target Customer:**
SMB to enterprise (broad — from Shopify merchants to Fortune 500s).

**Key Differentiator:**
Deep Shopify/PIM integrations + image editing (AI Batch Editor) alongside text enrichment. Long-standing market presence since 2020. 30+ language support.

**Threat Level: 🔴 DIRECT**
Core overlap with Fishbone: attribute extraction, product descriptions, catalog enrichment. Probably the most directly comparable tool in the market today.

---

### 2. Profound

**What they do:**
Enterprise AI brand visibility and monitoring platform (tryprofound.com). Tracks how brands and products appear across AI answer engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Gemini, Grok, etc.) and provides tools to optimize AI-generated representation.

**Positioning:**
"The AI visibility platform for enterprise marketing" — positioned as the next evolution of brand monitoring/SEO in an AI-first search world.

**Funding:**
$96M Series C at a $1B valuation. Clients include Target, Walmart, Indeed, MongoDB, Figma.

**Target Customer:**
Enterprise (Fortune 500 and large brands).

**Key Differentiator:**
"Profound Agents" for automated marketing orchestration; proprietary AI share-of-voice and citation analytics. Highest funding of any company in this layer.

**Threat Level: 🟡 ADJACENT**
Not catalog enrichment per se — they track/optimize how existing content appears in AI outputs. Relevant to Fishbone's long-term narrative (catalog enrichment → AI discoverability) but different tooling today. Could become a partner or complement.

---

### 3. Bluefish AI

**What they do:**
Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) platform for Fortune 500 brands. Monitors and optimizes how brands appear across generative AI platforms (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews). Emerged from stealth December 2024.

**Positioning:**
"The AI marketing platform for the agentic era" — enterprise-grade GEO with proprietary metrics (Impact Score, Influence Rank) and an "AI Commerce" module to increase product selection in agentic shopping flows.

**Funding:**
Series A — $20M led by NEA; Salesforce Ventures, Crane Venture Partners, Bloomberg Beta participating. ~$24M total disclosed.

**Target Customer:**
Fortune 500 / large enterprise. Clients: Adidas, Tishman Speyer.

**Key Differentiator:**
Strong adtech pedigree (founders from PromoteIQ/Microsoft, LiveRail/Facebook). Enterprise-grade brand analytics + AI commerce module targeting agentic purchasing flows.

**Threat Level: 🟡 ADJACENT**
Focus is on AI discovery and brand narrative, not catalog data enrichment. However, their "AI Commerce" module (optimizing product data for agentic selection) is drifting into Fishbone territory. One to monitor.

---

### 4. Peec AI

**What they do:**
Berlin-based AI search marketing / GEO platform. Helps brands track, analyze, and improve how they appear in AI-generated answers (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini). Founded February 2025 via Antler Berlin accelerator.

**Positioning:**
"The AI search marketing platform" — positioned as the GEO analytics layer, analogous to SEMrush but for AI search. Rapidly scaling: $4M+ ARR in 10 months, 1,300+ brands.

**Funding:**
€7M seed (July 2025) + $21M Series A led by Singular (November 2025). ~$29M total. Hypergrowth trajectory.

**Target Customer:**
Mid-market to enterprise brands and agencies. Clients: Axel Springer, Chanel, TUI, ElevenLabs.

**Key Differentiator:**
Speed of growth and GEO category leadership in Europe. Moving toward full "AI-era marketing stack" beyond analytics.

**Threat Level: 🟡 ADJACENT**
GEO/AI visibility monitoring rather than catalog enrichment. However, the positioning that "rich, structured product data drives AI discoverability" creates conceptual overlap with Fishbone's value proposition. Potential partnership angle (Fishbone enriches → Peec monitors visibility lift).

### 5. Merchkit

**What they do:**
Canadian AI platform for e-commerce catalog automation and merchandising. Extracts product data from web, spec sheets, and PDFs; auto-standardizes and enriches it; generates lifestyle images and channel-specific content for Shopify, Amazon, Walmart. Founded 2022.

**Positioning:**
"AI-powered merchandising that eliminates the grunt work" — positions as more than a PIM (active enrichment, not passive storage). Claims 90% efficiency gains and 50% cost reduction.

**Funding:**
Pre-seed — $2M CAD (~$1.4M USD) from Afore Capital and Antler Canada (announced March 2025).

**Target Customer:**
SMB to mid-market retailers and brands.

**Key Differentiator:**
Covers the full merchandising workflow (data extraction → enrichment → image/video generation → channel publishing). Broader scope than pure enrichment.

**Threat Level: 🔴 DIRECT**
Direct overlap with Fishbone: AI catalog enrichment, attribute extraction, standardization. Earlier stage but with similar mission and overlapping ICP.

### 6. Lily AI

**What they do:**
Product attribute enrichment platform for retail/fashion e-commerce. Extracts 15,000+ consumer-centric product attributes using computer vision, NLP, and deep learning. Bridges "brand-speak" to "customer-speak" to improve search, discovery, and personalization.

**Positioning:**
"The AI layer that connects products to shoppers" — focused on the fashion/apparel vertical with a heavy emphasis on how attribute enrichment drives search relevance and conversion.

**Funding:**
$62M total — $25M Series B (August 2022) + $20M Series B-1 (March 2024). Investors: Conductive Ventures, NEA, Canaan, Sorenson Capital, Cendana Capital.

**Target Customer:**
Enterprise retailers and brands (Macy's, Bloomingdale's, J.Crew, ThredUP, Abercrombie & Fitch).

**Key Differentiator:**
Vertical depth in fashion/apparel with the largest attribute taxonomy (15,000+ attributes). Proven enterprise relationships and significant funding.

**Threat Level: 🔴 DIRECT**
Core business is nearly identical to Fishbone's. Lily AI is the most funded and enterprise-entrenched direct competitor. Primarily fashion-focused, which may leave room for Fishbone in other verticals.

### 7. Nectar Social

**What they do:**
AI platform that transforms organic social channels into revenue engines for brands. Provides AI-powered community management, social listening, influencer tracking, and full-funnel revenue attribution for social commerce. Founded mid-2023.

**Positioning:**
"Turn social engagement into measurable revenue" — targeted at social-first DTC brands engaging Gen Z/Gen Alpha on Instagram, TikTok, etc.

**Funding:**
$10.6M seed (June 2025), co-led by True Ventures and GV (Google Ventures). Earlier $2M pre-seed from Flying Fish Ventures.

**Target Customer:**
Mid-market DTC/CPG brands. Early clients: OLIPOP, Jones Road Beauty, Solawave.

**Key Differentiator:**
AI agents trained on brand tone + customer history for personalized social engagement; full-funnel revenue attribution from organic social.

**Threat Level: 🟢 MINIMAL**
Social commerce is a distinct domain from catalog enrichment. No meaningful overlap with Fishbone's core offering. Possible adjacent relationship (enriched catalog data feeds social content), but not a competitor.

### 8. Novi (noviconnect.com)

**What they do:**
AI Shopping Optimizer that optimizes product data and trust signals (badges, certifications, endorsements) to increase product visibility and selection rates in AI-driven shopping (Google Gemini, ChatGPT, etc.). Launched AI Shopping Optimizer September 2025.

**Positioning:**
"Infrastructure for AI commerce" — helps brands ensure verified, optimized product data is consistently discoverable and selectable by AI agents. Frames their work as "AEO" (AI-driven E-commerce Optimization).

**Funding:**
Not publicly disclosed. Backed by Defy.vc (confirmed via their portfolio page).

**Target Customer:**
Mid-market to enterprise brands (Target, Ulta, Sephora referenced as distribution partners).

**Key Differentiator:**
Emphasis on "trust signals" and certification/retailer connectivity alongside product data — not just enrichment but credentialing for AI agents. Claims 230%+ lift in AI product selection.

**Threat Level: 🟡 ADJACENT**
Novi operates at the intersection of catalog data optimization and AI visibility — overlapping with both Fishbone's enrichment work and the GEO category. Not pure catalog enrichment, but their "product data for AI agents" framing is conceptually close. One to track.

Italian AI startup building a "composable AI operating system for e-commerce." Ingests raw product data, then validates, enriches, and transforms it into structured, machine-readable content for multiple markets and sales channels using specialized AI agents. Founded 2024.

**Positioning:**
"The AI OS for e-commerce catalog operations" — composed of specialized agents for catalog creation, updates, SEO optimization, and multi-channel distribution. Claims 90% cost reduction and 30% traffic/sales lift.

**Funding:**
€2.7M pre-seed (November 2025, oversubscribed). Investors: Italian Founders Fund (Koinos), Entourage, Redstone, Euregio+/Alpine VC, Vento (Exor), Ithaca, Vesper Holding.

**Target Customer:**
Mid-market and enterprise retailers (European focus). Clients: Nestlé Purina, Arcaplanet, Cisalfa, 1000Farmacie.

**Key Differentiator:**
Agentic architecture ("composable AI OS" framing). Strong founder pedigree (Amazon, Everli, Bain). European market focus with offices in Milan, Rome, London.

**Threat Level: 🔴 DIRECT**
Core mission nearly identical to Fishbone: raw product data → structured, enriched, multi-channel ready content. Different geography (EU-first) but same category and same "agentic catalog" narrative. Important to monitor as they expand.

### 10. ANGLERA

**What they do:**
YC-backed AI platform for product data enrichment, onboarding, and listing at scale. Processes product data from spreadsheets, PDFs, and websites to onboard new SKUs in seconds rather than weeks. Officially launched August 2025.

**Positioning:**
"Agentic commerce infrastructure" — positions product enrichment as foundational for a world where AI agents are the primary product discovery layer. Founders' background at Uber Eats (enriching millions of restaurant/product listings) informs their approach.

**Funding:**
$500K seed — YC (W25 or similar batch). Very early stage.

**Target Customer:**
SMB to mid-market brands, retailers, and marketplaces. Early clients: Jaapi, SecondShop, Arbor.

**Key Differentiator:**
YC backing + "agentic commerce" narrative positioning them as foundational infrastructure, not just a tool. Founder DNA from Uber Eats marketplace data operations. Lean and fast-moving.

**Threat Level: 🔴 DIRECT**
Nearly identical to Fishbone's core proposition: AI-powered product data enrichment for catalog readiness and AI agent discoverability. Both are early-stage, YC-adjacent. Most likely to be perceived as direct competitors in the same funding/customer conversations.

Catalog ads creative enrichment platform for Meta Dynamic Product Ads (DPA). Takes raw product feeds and transforms them into branded, styled catalog ad creatives — replacing ugly auto-generated thumbnails with on-brand visuals. 7,000+ marketers, $65M+/month in managed ad spend.

**Positioning:**
"The catalog creative layer" — focused on making catalog ads look like real branded ads rather than auto-generated product shots. Primary channel: Meta DPA.

**Funding:**
Not publicly disclosed at time of writing.

**Target Customer:**
DTC and e-commerce brands running Meta catalog/DPA campaigns.

**Key Differentiator:**
Specialization in the visual rendering layer of catalog ads. Deep Meta DPA integration. Scale (7K+ marketers).

**Threat Level: 🟡 ADJACENT**
Marpipe operates downstream of Fishbone — they take the product feed as-is and make the *creatives* look better. Fishbone works upstream on the *data quality* that feeds into those ads. Different layers of the same funnel. Not a direct competitor, but overlapping customer base (brands caring about catalog performance). Added per team request.

**Funding:**

## Key Takeaways for Fishbone

### Who to Watch Closest

**Tier 1 — Direct Competitors (immediate):**
- **ANGLERA** — Closest analog to Fishbone in mission, stage, and positioning. Both are building catalog enrichment infrastructure for the agentic commerce era. Will compete for same early customers and potentially same investor conversations. Monitor their YC batch positioning closely.
- **Lily AI** — Most funded direct competitor ($62M). Enterprise-entrenched with a proven attribute taxonomy in fashion. Risk is they expand verticals. Opportunity: they're expensive and fashion-specific; Fishbone can win in underserved verticals (home goods, electronics, B2B).
- **Hypotenuse AI** — Most mature direct competitor with broad market presence since 2020. They're the benchmark product feature comparison tool will use.
- **CommerceClarity** — Same "agentic catalog OS" narrative, EU-first. If Fishbone targets European retailers, direct collision. Alternatively: potential partnership or acqui-hire territory.

**Tier 2 — Adjacent but Converging:**
- **Bluefish AI** and **Profound** — Both building the "after the catalog" layer (AI visibility/GEO). As their platforms need clean, structured product data to work, **Fishbone is a natural upstream dependency**. Partnership angle: Fishbone enriches → Bluefish/Profound optimize visibility.
- **Novi** — Interesting model mixing product data optimization with trust signals. Their AEO framing overlaps with Fishbone's agentic narrative.

---

### Positioning Gaps & Opportunities

1. **Vertical specificity beyond fashion:** Lily AI owns fashion/apparel. The home goods, electronics, sporting goods, automotive parts, B2B industrial, and food/CPG verticals are underpenetrated by high-quality catalog enrichment. Fishbone can own a vertical.

2. **Agentic-first narrative:** While competitors are catching up, Fishbone can own the "catalog enrichment as AI agent infrastructure" frame more aggressively — enriched catalogs aren't just for search, they're the data layer that makes agentic commerce work. Most competitors frame this as a byproduct; Fishbone should make it the headline.

3. **Marketplace readiness:** None of the direct competitors prominently feature multi-marketplace listing optimization (Amazon, Walmart, Google Shopping, etc.) as a core use case. This is a strong motion: enrich once, publish to all channels with channel-specific formatting.

4. **Mid-market sweet spot:** Lily AI is enterprise-only. ANGLERA is very early and SMB-focused. Hypotenuse serves a very wide range (too generic). There's a clear mid-market gap (100–2,000 SKUs, $5M–$100M GMV brands) that Fishbone can own with focused go-to-market.

5. **Data provenance and accuracy:** As AI agents increasingly purchase on behalf of consumers, the accuracy and trustworthiness of product data becomes critical. Fishbone can differentiate on "verifiable, structured, agent-readable" catalog data — not just AI-generated text.

---

### Partnership Angles

- **GEO platforms (Profound, Bluefish, Peec AI):** Fishbone enriches the data upstream; GEO tools monitor its impact on AI visibility. A natural integration story. Co-sell potential: "enrich with Fishbone, measure lift with Peec."
- **Novi:** Their trust signals + product data optimization model is complementary to Fishbone's enrichment. Possible integration where Fishbone provides enriched attributes and Novi overlays credentialing.
- **Nectar Social:** Enriched product catalog data can power better social commerce content. Integration opportunity for social-first brands.
- **PIMs (Akeneo, Plytix, etc.):** Several competitors (Merch kit, CommerceClarity) position against PIM. Fishbone could instead partner with PIMs as the enrichment engine embedded within their workflows — a different GTM wedge.
