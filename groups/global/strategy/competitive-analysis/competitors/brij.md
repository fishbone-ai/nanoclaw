## Overview

QR-code-powered product experience platform that helps consumer brands capture first-party data from offline retail channels and build direct customer relationships. Brands embed QR codes on physical products to drive one-touch registration, reorders, subscriptions, and personalized digital experiences. Pivoting toward "agentic omnichannel engine" framing in 2025.

**Company:** BRIJ
**HQ:** USA
**Founded:** ~2019
**Platform:** Shopify, Klaviyo, and other e-commerce/marketing integrations
**Stage:** Seed ($8M, June 2025)

## Pricing

Not publicly disclosed.

## What they do

- QR code-driven product registration, warranty management, reorders, subscriptions
- First-party and zero-party data capture at SKU level from offline retail
- Customizable mobile-first product pages with content, tutorials, support
- AI engine for personalized post-purchase engagement
- Revenue attribution for offline customer relationships
- Digital product passport capabilities (EU regulatory compliance)
- "Agentic omnichannel engine" positioning — AI-driven personalization layer on top of physical products

## Strengths

- Unique offline → online data capture motion — no equivalent for physical retail
- Strong named customers (Heineken, Sapporo, Skullcandy)
- $8M raise signals investor confidence
- Digital product passport angle gives regulatory tailwind (EU)
- Shopify + Klaviyo integrations cover core DTC stack

## Weaknesses

- QR code adoption varies widely by category and retailer
- Not catalog enrichment — captures data post-sale, doesn't enrich product data pre-sale
- "Agentic omnichannel engine" claim feels aspirational vs. current product reality
- B2C/DTC focus limits B2B or marketplace applicability

## Threat level to Fishbone

**Minimal** — Different layer entirely. BRIJ is about post-purchase data capture from physical products; Fishbone is about pre-discovery catalog enrichment. No meaningful overlap today. Conceptually adjacent in the "product data quality" universe but solving different problems for different buyers.
