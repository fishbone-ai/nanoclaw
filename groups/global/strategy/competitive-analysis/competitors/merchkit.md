## Overview

Canadian AI platform for e-commerce catalog automation and merchandising. Extracts product data from web, spec sheets, and PDFs; auto-standardizes and enriches it; generates lifestyle images and channel-specific content for Shopify, Amazon, Walmart. Positions as "more than a PIM — active enrichment, not passive storage."

**Company:** Merchkit
**HQ:** Canada
**Founded:** 2022
**Platform:** Shopify, Amazon, Walmart, CSV
**Stage:** Pre-seed ($2M CAD from Afore Capital and Antler Canada, March 2025)

## Pricing

Not publicly disclosed.

## What they do

- Product data extraction from web pages, spec sheets, PDFs, supplier files
- AI-powered attribute standardization and enrichment
- Lifestyle image and product image generation
- Channel-specific content formatting (Shopify, Amazon, Walmart requirements)
- Catalog publishing across sales channels
- Claims 90% efficiency gains and 50% cost reduction for merchandising teams

## Strengths

- Full merchandising workflow coverage (ingest → enrich → image generation → publish)
- Multi-channel publishing built-in
- Practical founder positioning — solves real operational pain, not just AI hype
- Backed by Afore Capital (known for early-stage Canadian bets)

## Weaknesses

- Very early stage — pre-seed, limited market traction evidence
- Small Canadian startup competing against better-funded US players
- Broad scope (enrichment + images + publishing) may dilute product depth
- No agentic-commerce or AI discoverability framing apparent

## Threat level to Fishbone

**Direct** — Same core problem: AI catalog enrichment and multi-channel readiness. Earlier stage and Canadian-focused, but essentially the same mission. Will likely compete for the same SMB/mid-market deals.
