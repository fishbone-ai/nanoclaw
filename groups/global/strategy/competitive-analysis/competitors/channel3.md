## Overview

Building a universal product database — every product on the internet, made discoverable by AI agents. Uses multimodal AI to extract product attributes, match products across merchants, and serve a real-time catalog API for developers building AI shopping applications. YC-backed, $6M seed led by Matrix with Paul Graham as angel. 50M+ products as of Dec 2025.

**Company:** channel3
**HQ:** USA
**Founded:** 2025
**Platform:** API (developer-focused)
**Stage:** Seed ($6M led by Matrix, Dec 2025; angels: Paul Graham, Sri Batchu, Matteo Franceschetti)

## Pricing

API access — pricing not publicly disclosed.

## What they do

- Universal product catalog: indexes every product on the internet for AI agent discovery
- Multimodal AI for product attribute extraction, cross-merchant product matching, variant linking
- Real-time product catalog API for developers building AI shopping apps
- Structured product data layer enabling AI agents to find/recommend products regardless of listing format
- Powers AI shopping applications: stylists, interior design tools, shopping assistants
- Storefront-agnostic — merchants don't need deep technical integration to appear

## Strengths

- YC-backed + Paul Graham as angel — exceptional credibility signal
- Matrix-led Series A pipeline essentially guaranteed at this trajectory
- 50M+ product catalog in less than a year — extraordinary scale for seed stage
- Infrastructure play — if they become the standard AI-product-database, enormous defensibility
- Developer-facing API creates ecosystem effects (more apps built on it → more merchants want to be in it)
- Founders recognized a genuine gap: AI agents can't shop effectively because structured product data doesn't exist at scale

## Weaknesses

- Very early stage — seed with no public revenue metrics
- Infrastructure bets take time to monetize
- Aggregating product data at scale raises potential legal/IP questions with merchants
- Dependent on developer ecosystem adoption — chicken-and-egg problem

## Threat level to Fishbone

**Direct (strategic)** — channel3 is building what could become the foundational layer for agentic commerce product data. If they succeed, every product needs to be in their database — and catalog enrichment (Fishbone's work) becomes a prerequisite for appearing there well. This creates both a threat (if channel3 builds enrichment in-house) and a partnership angle (Fishbone enriches data that flows into channel3). The relationship to watch most carefully.
