# eLLMo (tryellmo.ai)

Last updated: 2026-03-28

## What they do

"The Catalog Layer for Agentic Commerce" -- eLLMo ingests brand/merchant catalog data, structures it into agent-readable representations (nodes, attributes, use-cases, JSON-LD), and makes it discoverable across AI shopping surfaces: Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, and Perplexity.

Their pitch: AI agents see structure, not photos. eLLMo translates your product catalog into the structured representation that agents use to match intent and complete transactions.

## Key features

- **PDP agent audit** -- free tool: paste a product URL, they run it through 5 agent models and return a structured analysis (optimization gaps, missing structured data, attribute completeness)
- **Multi-model encoding** -- vision, symbolic reasoning, semantic embedding, commerce classification, unified meta-representation
- **Live agent activity feed** -- shows in real-time which AI agents (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI) are recommending, skipping, or purchasing from your catalog
- **Optimization gap detection** -- flags missing structured data, unclear material composition, absent care instructions, etc.
- **Google AI Overviews** -- specific focus on organic AI visibility (not Shopping ads), schema/structured data as primary ranking signal

## Framing

They describe the layer they're building as an "agentic commerce trust layer" / "product truth" provider. Their tagline emphasizes that what the merchant sees is a photo -- what an agent sees is structure. The whole pitch is about translating your existing catalog into agent-readable format.

## Positioning

- Competing on the same layer as Fishbone: feed structuring → AI visibility
- More explicit about the agentic commerce framing than most competitors
- The free audit tool (PDP → 5-model analysis) is a clever lead gen / awareness play
- Not selling a PIM or a review platform -- purely the catalog-to-agent translation layer

## ICP / pricing

- Unknown pricing (not listed publicly)
- Appears to target DTC/mid-market brands based on UI and messaging (not enterprise-heavy)
- Free audit tool suggests a self-serve or product-led entry motion

## Strengths

- Strong framing -- "what an agent sees is structure" is a clear, sharp pitch
- Free audit tool lowers barrier to engagement and generates pipeline
- Multi-surface coverage (ChatGPT + Perplexity + Google AI Overviews)
- Agent activity feed is a compelling visual proof point

## Weaknesses / open questions

- Funding status unknown
- Team background unknown

## Threat level

**High** -- direct competitor building the same catalog-to-AI-agent structuring layer. Their framing overlaps with Fishbone's thesis. Watch their commercialization closely.
