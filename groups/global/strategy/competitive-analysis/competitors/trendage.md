## Overview

AI-powered fashion data and styling platform that generates product recommendations based on community style preferences, visual search, and trend data. Founded 2015, launched automated recommendations in 2018. Primarily a fashion/apparel vertical play combining AI product tagging, outfit recommendations, and generative AI for catalog imagery and classification.

**Company:** Trendage, Inc.
**HQ:** Santa Clara, CA, USA
**Founded:** 2015
**Platform:** API integrations with fashion/apparel retailers
**Stage:** Unknown — older company, funding not publicly disclosed

## Pricing

Not publicly disclosed.

## What they do

- AI style recommendations — suggests product pairings, complete-the-look outfits
- Visual search and community-driven style matching
- Generative AI for catalog: outfitting models, product classification, new product photo generation
- Consumer-facing "Style Challenge" app — gathers style preference data
- Personalization for product pages and email campaigns based on shopper age and region
- Machine learning models for fashion attribute extraction and tagging

## Strengths

- Long track record in fashion AI (since 2015) — data moat from years of style preference collection
- Generative AI for product imagery — differentiator in catalog photo production
- Consumer-facing data collection via Style Challenge provides proprietary training data
- Fashion/apparel vertical depth

## Weaknesses

- Older company with unknown funding — unclear if they're growing or plateauing
- Fashion-vertical specific — limited applicability outside apparel/accessories
- Not primarily a catalog enrichment play — recommendations and imagery, not structured attribute enrichment
- No obvious agentic commerce positioning

## Threat level to Fishbone

**Minimal** — Fashion-specific and focused on styling/recommendations/imagery rather than catalog attribute enrichment. If Fishbone targets the fashion/apparel vertical, Trendage's visual AI and attribute tagging capabilities become more relevant to compare. For now, different enough that they're not a direct concern.
