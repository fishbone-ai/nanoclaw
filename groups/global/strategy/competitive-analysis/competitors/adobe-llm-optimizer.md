## Overview

Enterprise-grade GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) platform. Helps brands monitor and improve how they appear in AI-generated answers across ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Copilot, and Google AI Overviews.

This is Adobe's answer to "how do brands show up in AI search." It's not a startup -- it's a product from a $200B+ company with deep enterprise distribution.

**Parent company:** Adobe
**Announced:** June 2025 (Cannes Lions)
**GA launch:** October 2025
**Target:** Enterprise brands ($100M+ revenue)

## Pricing

- **Entry point:** ~$115,000/year for 1,000 tracked prompts
- **Scaling formula:** Markets x Products x Topics = Total Prompts
- **Larger deployments:** $765,000+/year
- **Increments:** 200 prompts
- Enterprise sales motion -- no self-serve

This is firmly enterprise pricing. Mid-market and SMB merchants are priced out.

## What they do

Four core capabilities:

### 1. Brand presence intelligence
- Track mentions across ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews
- Visibility score, mentions, citations, sentiment
- Weekly refresh (daily available for extra cost)

### 2. Agentic traffic insights
- Analyze CDN logs to see how AI bots crawl your site
- URL-level performance metrics standard analytics miss
- Identify which pages influence AI responses

### 3. Referral attribution
- Connect AI citations to actual visits and engagement
- Integration with Adobe Analytics planned
- Move beyond vanity metrics to business outcomes

### 4. Optimization engine ("Optimize at Edge")
- Prescriptive recommendations with one-click CDN deployment
- Pre-render JavaScript-heavy pages for AI crawlers
- Serve optimized HTML at CDN layer without changing origin CMS
- Remove crawler blockers
- "Make any website agentic-ready in seconds"

## How they compare to us

Adobe LLM Optimizer is a **monitoring and visibility** tool for big brands. It answers "are AI agents finding us?" and helps optimize existing content for discoverability. It does NOT do catalog enrichment.

**Key differences from Fishbone:**
- They optimize existing content visibility -- we create/improve the content itself
- They monitor brand mentions in AI -- we fix the underlying product data
- They're enterprise-only ($115k+/year) -- we target mid-market
- They work at the CDN/markup layer -- we work at the product data layer
- They're platform-agnostic (any website) -- we're commerce-specific

**Where they overlap:**
- Both care about AI agent discoverability
- Both touch product page optimization
- "Edge optimization" for product pages is adjacent to catalog enrichment
- Their customers are exactly the type who'd also need enrichment upstream

## Strengths

- Adobe brand and enterprise distribution -- instant credibility
- Deep technical moat (CDN-level optimization, Adobe Analytics integration)
- First mover in enterprise GEO
- Huge marketing budget to educate the market on AI visibility
- Platform-agnostic -- works with any CMS
- Customer example: Frescopa saw 500% increase in AI citations within one week

## Weaknesses (from our perspective)

- Enterprise-only pricing ($115k+) leaves mid-market wide open
- Monitoring and optimization, not enrichment -- doesn't fix bad product data
- Requires existing content worth optimizing -- garbage in, polished garbage out
- No commerce-specific features (no understanding of SKUs, attributes, taxonomy)
- Complex enterprise sales cycle
- Governance gaps (no role-based permissions)
- Data locked to AWS us-east-1

## Assessment

Adobe is educating the market on AI visibility, which benefits us. Every enterprise CMO who hears about LLM Optimizer will start thinking about AI discoverability -- and many will realize their underlying product data isn't good enough to optimize. That's our opening.

Adobe is not a direct competitor to Fishbone. They're upstream (visibility monitoring) of what we do (data enrichment). In fact, they could be a demand generation engine for us: "Adobe told me I'm not showing up in AI search" leads naturally to "my catalog data needs to be better."

The real risk is if Adobe builds enrichment into Commerce Optimizer or adds catalog-specific features to LLM Optimizer. Worth watching, but enterprise Adobe moves slowly.
