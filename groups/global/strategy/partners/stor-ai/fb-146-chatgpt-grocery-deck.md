## SLIDE 6 — What the App Actually Does

**Subtitle:** This isn't a chatbot. It's your store, inside the AI assistant your customers already trust.

**How it works (3 steps):**

1. **Customer asks ChatGPT** — "Help me plan dinners for this week" / "I need ingredients for shakshuka"
2. **Your branded app activates** — real-time catalog, your prices, your stock, your brand
3. **Customer clicks through to your site** — checkout on your platform, in your ecosystem

**What makes it yours, not generic:**

- **Connected account (OAuth)** — Customer signs in with their existing account. Their order history, preferences, loyalty status — all flow into the conversation.
- **Real-time catalog** — powered by Stor.AI's live inventory. What's in stock. What's on offer. What's in their local branch.
- **Checkout stays on your platform** — the final transaction happens on your website. You keep the customer relationship, the data, the loyalty points.
- **Personalized intelligence** — alternative products, upsell, substitutions — powered by your actual catalog logic, not a generic AI guess.

**Key message:**
> The customer never leaves your ecosystem. They start on ChatGPT, they end on your checkout. The AI is the front door. You own the house.

## SLIDE 7 — A New Class of Customer Intelligence

**Subtitle:** For the first time, you're not just seeing what customers bought — you're seeing what they wanted.

**What traditional channels give you:**
- Click data
- Purchase data
- Abandonment rates

**What the ChatGPT channel gives you — for the first time ever:**
- **Unmet demand:** "Do you have gluten-free pasta under ₪20?" → You see a gap before it costs you a basket
- **Frustration signals:** Repeated queries for out-of-stock items → real-time inventory intelligence
- **Meal planning intent:** Full weekly menu requests → predictive basket modeling, upsell opportunities
- **Natural language preferences:** "Something my kids will actually eat" → qualitative insight no survey captures
- **Seasonal intent clustering:** Queries spike around holidays before purchases do → forecast signal

**The insight:**
> Natural language prompts are a direct line to customer intent. No click stream, no survey, no focus group has ever given you this. This channel is a data asset as much as a commerce channel.

## SLIDE 8 — This Is Not a DIY Project

**Subtitle:** What looks like "just connecting a chatbot" is actually a layered technical challenge. Walmart learned this the hard way.

**Why the complexity is real:**

| Challenge | What it actually requires |
|---|---|
| Real-time catalog accuracy | Live API sync, not a static feed. Stock changes per branch, per hour. |
| Personalization via OAuth | Secure account linking, order history integration, preference modeling |
| Conversation context management | Multi-turn state: the user asked about pasta 3 messages ago — the app needs to remember |
| Product intelligence | Substitutions, alternatives, upsells — requires category logic, not just search |
| Platform compliance | ChatGPT's app approval process, content policies, tool call schemas |
| Multi-platform readiness | Built once on MCP — deployable to Gemini when the surface matures |

**The Instacart CTO said it clearly:**

## SLIDE 10 — Addressing Concerns

**Q: Will customers actually use this?**
64% of consumers plan to use AI for shopping in 2026. ChatGPT is the #1 AI app in Israel (+227% growth). The behavior is already shifting.

**Q: What if ChatGPT changes the rules?**
OpenAI shifted *toward* retailer apps in March 2026 — not away. The architecture (MCP) is an open standard adopted across platforms. The same app runs on Gemini.

**Q: What about Google / Gemini?**
Every major retailer is doing both. Loblaw launched on ChatGPT in February 2026 and announced a Gemini partnership the same month. We build on MCP — extends to Gemini when ready.

**Q: What about data and privacy?**
Customer PII stays on your infrastructure and OpenAI's. Fishbone handles query content and catalog responses — not personal data. Checkout happens on your platform.

**Q: What if we want to stop?**
No lock-in. No infrastructure to unwind. Low switching cost, no capital at risk. Low-risk experiment with asymmetric upside.

**Q: Can we build this ourselves?**
Walmart tried the generic path and switched after poor results. Building it well requires catalog integration expertise (Stor.AI), MCP development experience, and ChatGPT app approval know-how. We've combined all three. Time to market for you: weeks vs. 6–12 months from scratch.
