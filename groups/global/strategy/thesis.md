# Fishbone Thesis

E-commerce is drowning in bad product data. Merchants have hundreds or thousands of SKUs with thin descriptions, missing attributes, and inconsistent formatting. This directly hurts discovery, conversion, and returns. The problem gets worse as catalogs grow, and most teams don't have the bandwidth to fix it manually.

Fishbone uses AI agents to enrich product catalogs at scale. We take existing product data -- titles, images, specs, supplier feeds -- and generate rich, accurate, conversion-optimized content. Better descriptions. Complete attributes. Structured data that makes products findable and buyable.

We're starting with catalog enrichment because it's concrete, measurable, and delivers value before anything else needs to change. A merchant can see better product pages within days, not months. From there, we expand into the broader agentic commerce stack: smart search, personalized recommendations, and eventually autonomous purchasing agents that need high-quality product data to function.

The bet: whoever owns the product data layer becomes essential infrastructure for the next generation of commerce. Clean, rich, structured catalog data is the foundation everything else gets built on.

We're going after mid-market merchants first. Big enough catalogs to feel the pain, small enough teams to appreciate the leverage.
