## Overview

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) platform focused on e-commerce catalog visibility in AI-generated search results. Helps brands restructure product information architecture so AI platforms (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) can better understand, categorize, and recommend their products. Positioned as a managed service + SaaS hybrid. Named "Best Generative Engine Optimization Service Provider" in an industry award.

**Company:** Algomizer
**HQ:** Israel
**Founded:** ~2014 (pivoted to GEO/AI visibility focus by 2024-2025)
**Platform:** Platform-agnostic (web/catalog consulting + SaaS)
**Stage:** Unknown — older company that pivoted; funding not publicly disclosed

## Pricing

Not publicly disclosed. Appears to offer both SaaS and managed service tiers.

## What they do

- Catalog optimization for AI search comprehensibility — restructures product data architecture
- Category mapping aligned with how consumers query AI systems
- Structured data markup implementation for product pages
- AI visibility tracking — monitors brand mentions and rankings in AI-generated answers
- Competitive differentiation framing for each product category
- Information consistency systems across AI platforms
- Claimed case study results: increased AI mention scores, expanded category coverage, higher conversion from AI-referred traffic

## Strengths

- Established company with long operational history (pivoted into GEO)
- Award recognition in GEO category
- Israeli market knowledge and team
- Both consulting and SaaS offering gives flexibility

## Weaknesses

- Older company pivoting into a new category — not native to agentic commerce
- GEO analytics/recommendations, not catalog enrichment generation
- Less clear product differentiation vs. Profound, Peec AI, Adobe
- Limited public traction data or notable named customers

## Threat level to Fishbone

**Adjacent** — GEO optimization framing, not catalog data enrichment. Algomizer helps products get found in AI results but doesn't generate or restructure the underlying catalog data the way Fishbone does. The conceptual overlap is in the "catalog data quality drives AI discoverability" thesis, but the execution is different.
