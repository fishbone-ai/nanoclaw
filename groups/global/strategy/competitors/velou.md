# Velou

Last updated: 2026-03-24

## Links

- **Website:** https://www.velou.com/

## Overview

Catalog enrichment platform for agentic commerce. Built around a proprietary retail AI model ("Commerce-1") trained exclusively on retail data, claiming 99.8% accuracy where generic models fail. Positions as the data infrastructure layer for the next era of AI agent-driven commerce.

**HQ:** Unknown
**Stage:** Unknown (enterprise-scale, 100M+ products processed)
**Funding:** Unknown
**Target:** Enterprise retailers and brands

## What they do

### Core capabilities

- **Attribute enrichment** — extracts and generates missing attributes, standardized tags, and rich content from raw text and imagery
- **Product graphs** — maps relationships between products (not just tagging in isolation), enabling AI agents to reason about alternatives, compatibility, and "complete the look" bundles
- **Structured data generation** — formats data for AI agent consumption (ChatGPT-style agents, voice search, etc.)
- **Brand-safe inference** — ensures generated content stays on-brand at scale

### Use cases

- **AI & Voice Discovery** — makes SKUs readable by ChatGPT-style agents, protecting organic reach
- **Performance Marketing** — smarter product titles for PMAX, Shopping Ads, social DPAs
- **On-Site Search & Merchandising** — attribute-rich catalogs power search facets and recommendations
- **Marketplaces & Comparison Shopping** — fewer disapprovals, better rank, more buy-box wins

### Commerce-1 model

Their proprietary model is trained exclusively on retail. Main pitch: generic LLMs hallucinate on product data; Commerce-1 doesn't. Trusted across 100M+ products — their main moat claim.

## How they compare to Fishbone

**Direct overlaps:**
- Product attribute enrichment & structured data generation
- Performance marketing (PMAX, Shopping Ads, GMC optimization)
- Making catalogs readable by AI agents (ChatGPT, voice, etc.)
- Marketplace listing quality

**Where they're stronger:**
- Commerce-1 proprietary model is a genuine moat — years of retail-specific training data
- Enterprise scale (100M+ products) — infrastructure is proven
- Product graphs are a differentiating capability — relational context, not just per-SKU tagging
- "Agentic commerce" is their core thesis, not a layer on top of an SEO product

**Where Fishbone has opportunity:**
- Velou appears enterprise-only — no visible self-serve, SMB, or Shopify app entry point
- Mid-market merchants with smaller catalogs are likely priced out
- Unknown funding/stage means possible go-to-market gaps
- Fishbone can move faster and iterate on specific verticals

## Strengths

- Proprietary retail AI model (Commerce-1) — strongest accuracy claim in the space
- Product graph approach is architecturally differentiated — relational intelligence vs. flat enrichment
- Agentic commerce framing is explicit and consistent — not bolted on
- Platform-agnostic, pushes enriched data into existing infrastructure

## Weaknesses

- No visible pricing or self-serve — enterprise-only motion likely
- No known customers named publicly
- Funding, stage, and team are opaque — hard to assess velocity
- Product graph complexity may mean longer implementation cycles

## Assessment

Velou is a direct and credible competitor. Their Commerce-1 model and product graph approach make them technically differentiated, not just another enrichment tool. The "agentic commerce" framing is identical to what Fishbone should own. The key question is whether they're moving fast on go-to-market or still primarily technical/infrastructure-focused. Their enterprise-only positioning leaves mid-market open. Worth tracking closely — especially any announcements on funding, named customers, or a self-serve tier.
