## Overview

Enterprise Product Experience Management (PXM) platform. Competes directly with Salsify in the PIM space. Offers a broader "Product Cloud" suite: PIM, DAM (Digital Asset Management), SDM (Supplier Data Management), Activation (syndication), Digital Showroom, and PX Insights. Has a free trial, which makes them more accessible than Salsify at the low end.

Founded in France (Nantes), now a global company. Open-source roots — originally built a community edition that drove wide adoption.

**HQ:** Nantes, France + Boston, MA
**Founded:** 2013
**Funding:** $120M+ (Series D, 2021)
**Target:** Mid-market to enterprise brands, manufacturers, retailers
**Key integrations:** Shopify, Adobe, Salesforce, SAP

## What they do

### Product Information Management (PIM)
- Central product data management and governance
- Multi-channel, multi-locale product content management
- Workflow and approval processes for content teams

### Digital Asset Management (DAM)
- Manage product images, videos, documents alongside product data
- Link assets directly to product records

### Supplier Data Management (SDM)
- Onboard supplier product data at scale
- Normalize and validate incoming supplier content

### Activation (Syndication)
- Distribute enriched product data to retail channels and marketplaces
- Multi-platform output with channel-specific formatting

### PX Insights
- Customer data-driven optimization of product experiences
- Feedback loop from commerce performance back into PIM

### AI Features
- AI enrichment capabilities built into the platform
- Auto-categorization and attribute suggestions
- Natural language product descriptions (announced/rolling out)
- Positioned as "product data that powers commerce" with AI as an enabler

## How they compare to us

Similar story to Salsify — Akeneo is a PIM/governance layer, not a purpose-built enrichment tool. But they're adding AI features and have a broader, more accessible market profile.

**Overlap:**
- Both work at the product data layer
- Akeneo's AI enrichment features are growing — convergence risk
- Describely actually lists Akeneo as a connector (you can push Describely outputs into Akeneo) — shows Akeneo as a "destination," not a competitor to enrichment tools

**Differences:**
- Akeneo is a system of record; Fishbone is a value-add layer on top
- Free trial lowers the barrier — they serve smaller businesses than Salsify
- Their SDM (Supplier Data Management) is unique — enrichment before data even reaches the PIM
- Open-source community edition gave them wide distribution and developer affinity

**Partnership angle:** Akeneo is explicitly integrated with Describely — meaning enrichment tools feeding into Akeneo is an established pattern. Fishbone could position as "enrich your catalog, push to Akeneo" — making us a complement, not a threat.

**Convergence risk:** If Akeneo's AI enrichment goes from classification/auto-fill to generative AEO content, they have the distribution to reach thousands of mid-market brands with it bundled. That's a real threat.

## Strengths

- Broader platform than Salsify (PIM + DAM + SDM + Activation in one)
- Open-source heritage = developer community and word-of-mouth
- More mid-market accessible (free trial, lower price floor)
- Major platform integrations (Shopify, Adobe, Salesforce, SAP) = deep in existing stacks
- Strong European presence (France-founded, EU retailer base)

## Weaknesses

- AI features still appear to be classification/automation, not generative AEO content
- Complex platform — implementation still requires SI partners
- European focus may limit US enterprise traction
- PIM buying motion is different from enrichment buying motion — different budget owners

## Assessment

Akeneo is closer to mid-market than Salsify, which makes them slightly more relevant as a potential threat. But they're still a governance/management platform, not an enrichment-first product. The Describely integration pattern shows there's room for an enrichment layer that sits on top of PIM systems — Fishbone can own that position.

The risk is the same as Salsify: AI enrichment bundled into PIM removes the need for a standalone enrichment tool. Monitor their AI roadmap quarterly.
