## Overview

Agentic commerce infrastructure. One integration that connects a merchant's commerce stack to every AI platform -- ChatGPT, Gemini, shopping agents, agentic browsers. Their core product is **AgentLane**, a middleware layer between merchants and AI surfaces.

**HQ:** NYC (founded in Israel based on team)
**Founded:** ~2024
**Funding:** $5M seed (May 2025), led by Madrona, with Visa Ventures and Amex Ventures
**Key person:** Ayal Karmi (CEO)
**Platform:** Multi-platform (SAP, WooCommerce, Adobe Commerce, Salesforce)

## What they do

Nekuda sits in a different layer than 40rty AI. They're not about catalog optimization -- they're about the commerce infrastructure that lets AI agents actually transact.

### AgentLane (core product)

- **Universal checkout layer** -- one checkout powers every AI surface
- **Access control & permissioning** -- API key management with rate limits, scopes, audit logging
- **Intelligence layer** -- unified analytics across AI channels, conversion funnels, dashboards
- **Observability** -- system health monitoring, automated alerts

### SDK (payments)

- Secure wallet for AI agents to collect, store, and use payment credentials
- Handles PCI compliance so merchants/agent builders don't have to
- Supports network tokens and card network requirements
- Use cases: browser assistants, in-chat purchasing, voice-driven purchases

### Developer tools (free/open-source)

- Protocol Scout -- tracks agentic commerce protocols
- Checkout.directory -- merchant directory
- ACP Checkout Gateway -- open-source implementation
- ACP Validator-CLI

## How they're different from us

Nekuda is **infrastructure**, not **catalog enrichment**. They solve "how does an AI agent complete a purchase?" -- we solve "how does an AI agent find and understand your products?"

They're actually **complementary** to Fishbone, not competitive:
- Nekuda needs merchants with good product data for agents to discover -- that's what we provide
- A merchant could use Fishbone to enrich their catalog, then Nekuda to enable agent checkout
- They don't touch product data quality at all

## Why they matter to us

1. **They validate the agentic commerce thesis** with serious backing (Visa, Amex, Madrona)
2. **They're building the transaction layer** -- if agents can buy, they need good product data to know *what* to buy
3. **Their investor roster signals where the market is going** -- payment networks betting on agents
4. **Potential partnership angle** -- enriched catalog + checkout infrastructure = complete agentic commerce stack
5. **Their Substack is a goldmine** for market intelligence on protocols (ACP, UCP, MCP) and platform moves

## Strengths

- Serious investors (Visa, Amex, Madrona) -- institutional validation
- Multi-platform from day one (SAP, WooCommerce, Adobe, Salesforce)
- Open-source tools building developer community
- Deep protocol expertise (ACP, UCP, MCP)
- Positioned at the payment/checkout layer -- hard to displace once embedded

## Weaknesses (from our perspective)

- Not a direct competitor -- they don't touch catalog data quality
- Their success depends on merchants having good enough product data for agents to work with
- Enterprise-focused, which may leave SMB/mid-market underserved
- Complex infrastructure play -- longer sales cycles
