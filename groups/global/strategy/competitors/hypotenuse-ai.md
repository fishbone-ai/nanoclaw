# Hypotenuse AI

Last updated: 2026-03-10

## Links

- **Website:** https://hypotenuse.ai/

## Overview

AI-powered e-commerce catalog enrichment platform that generates product descriptions, fills missing attributes, standardizes data, and provides bulk image editing. Founded 2020, YC-backed. One of the longest-standing players in AI catalog content generation.

**Company:** Hypotenuse AI
**HQ:** Singapore
**Founded:** 2020
**Platform:** Shopify, WooCommerce, PIM integrations (Akeneo, etc.), CSV
**Stage:** Seed (YC graduate)

## Pricing

Not publicly disclosed for enterprise tiers. SMB plans available via website. Estimated ARR ~$2.8M as of early 2026.

## What they do

- AI product description generation at scale (bulk, SEO-optimized)
- Attribute extraction and gap-filling from existing product data
- Catalog data standardization and enrichment
- Bulk AI image editing (background removal, resizing, lifestyle image generation)
- 30+ language support for international catalogs
- PIM and Shopify integrations for direct publish workflows
- "HypoChat" for conversational catalog queries

## Strengths

- Longest market presence in AI catalog content (since 2020) — most mature product
- Broad integration surface (Shopify, WooCommerce, Akeneo, CSV)
- Strong SEO-optimization angle appeals to broad buyer base
- 30+ language support for global retailers
- YC backing gives credibility and network
- Wide ICP from SMBs to Fortune 500s

## Weaknesses

- Generalist positioning — "AI content for e-commerce" competes on price and breadth, not depth
- Not explicitly agentic-commerce-first; framing is still SEO-first
- No evidence of structured attribute taxonomy at Lily AI depth
- SMB to enterprise breadth means diluted focus; may lack enterprise-grade data governance
- Older codebase relative to newer agentic-first startups

## Threat level to Fishbone

**Direct** — Core overlap: attribute extraction, product descriptions, catalog enrichment. Hypotenuse is the benchmark most prospects will compare Fishbone against on day one. Their broad market presence and integrations are the main threat; their generic positioning is the opening.
