## What they do

Trustana is an AI-powered product data enrichment and transformation platform targeting mid-market and enterprise retailers, brand owners, and distributors. Their core pitch: AI ROI fails because of weak product data, not weak models. "AI is a multiplier, not a fix."

**Product suite:**
- **Unify** -- ingest and harmonize product data from fragmented sources
- **Enrich** -- AI-powered attribute extraction, description generation, image processing
- **Activate** -- push enriched data to commerce channels and feeds
- **Onboarder** -- rapid SKU onboarding tool (5-day setup, supplier-feed focused)

Tech stack: OCR, computer vision, NLP for enrichment. ML for speed, human validation for quality. Not fully agentic.

Also powers **supplier-facing enrichment portals** for large retailers (e.g., David Jones supplier portal).

---

## Their framework: The 5 Pillars of AI Readiness

Core sales narrative used in all content and demos:

1. **Completeness** -- all required attributes present
2. **Accuracy** -- correct and standardized values
3. **Consistency** -- uniform taxonomy and schema across SKUs, categories, and channels
4. **Imagery** -- high-quality, correctly tagged product images (multi-view, lifestyle, color-matched)
5. **Governance** -- ongoing processes, audits, roles to maintain standards

Business outcome mapping:
- Completeness -> higher PDP conversion, fewer abandoned carts
- Accuracy -> fewer returns
- Consistency -> better search visibility
- Imagery -> higher visual-first discovery engagement
- Governance -> reduced risk, faster launches, scalable growth

## Where they fall short

- **Reactive, not proactive.** Positioning is entirely about cleaning up existing messes. No framing around preventing data debt or autonomous catalog maintenance.
- **Sales-led only.** Primea required a 5-day setup and human onboarding. Fishbone could offer instant self-serve for the same ICP.
- **Not agentic.** ML + human validation = supervised enrichment, not autonomous operation.
- **Project-oriented, not infrastructure.** Runs as a managed process (audit -> enrich -> govern -> repeat). Not a platform that self-manages.
- **Metrics are backward-looking.** They measure time-to-market reduction and manual effort saved -- not AI agent visibility or agentic commerce readiness.

## Fishbone differentiation

Trustana's ceiling: data humans keep clean with AI assistance.
Fishbone's bet: a catalog that maintains itself.

Their Primea case study is the clearest signal: a solo founder spending 200 days on manual SKU data -- Trustana required a 5-day setup and a sales engagement to solve this. Fishbone's answer should be self-serve, instant, and autonomous.

Their ebook validates Fishbone's core thesis verbatim: "AI-powered agents shopping on behalf of customers will only consider products with complete structured data." They're preparing customers for that world. Fishbone is that world.
