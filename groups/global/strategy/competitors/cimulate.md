Last updated: 2026-03-10

## Overview

AI-native commerce platform focused on product search, discovery, and conversational shopping. Built CommerceGPT (context-aware search and merchandising) and CustomerGPT (conversational shopping assistant). **Acquired by Salesforce on February 12, 2026** and integrated into Salesforce Agentforce Commerce platform. No longer an independent competitor.

**Company:** Cimulate, Inc.
**HQ:** USA
**Founded:** ~2022
**Platform:** API + MCP server, integrated into Salesforce Commerce Cloud
**Stage:** Acquired (raised $33.7M total: $5.2M seed 2023 + $28.5M Series A Dec 2024 led by Spark Capital)

## Pricing

No longer independently available — integrated into Salesforce Agentforce Commerce.

## What they do (pre-acquisition)

- CommerceGPT: AI-native product search tailored to each retailer's catalog and customer behavior
- CustomerGPT: Conversational shopping assistant simulating millions of shopping journeys
- Commerce Experience Server: hyper-optimized content and context-aware search delivery
- Cimulate Browse: AI-driven category pages that reorganize in real-time based on behavior/trends
- Automated merchandising and catalog ranking optimization
- MCP server integration for AI-native commerce experiences

## Strengths

- Acquired by Salesforce — highest-signal validation possible; tech was strong enough for Salesforce to buy
- Series A led by Spark Capital — top-tier investor
- CommerceGPT approach (per-retailer custom model) showed deep product thinking
- MCP integration was ahead of the curve

## Weaknesses (pre-acquisition)

- Search/discovery focus rather than catalog data enrichment — different problem
- Enterprise-only play
- Now part of Salesforce, so not accessible to merchants outside the Salesforce Commerce Cloud ecosystem

## Threat level to Fishbone

**Minimal (post-acquisition)** — As a Salesforce product, Cimulate is now part of a much larger platform play. For merchants on Salesforce Commerce Cloud, this is a bundled feature, not a purchase decision. For everyone else, it's irrelevant. The acquisition does validate the "AI-native catalog intelligence" category broadly, which is a positive signal for Fishbone.

**Notable signal:** Salesforce paying to acquire this capability confirms that AI-powered catalog intelligence is a feature enterprises want embedded in their commerce stack. Fishbone's angle is to be the standalone, platform-agnostic version.
