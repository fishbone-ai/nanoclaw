**HQ:** Lewes, Delaware
**Founded:** 2026
**Size:** 2-10 employees
**LinkedIn followers:** 5
**Funding:** Unknown (appears bootstrapped or very early)

## What they do

- Create AI-native product catalogs discoverable by AI agents
- Semantic search and ranking engine for product data
- Convert unstructured product data into machine-readable formats
- Integration with AI chatbots and social commerce platforms
- PIM synchronization and structured data ingestion

Their pitch: "Business solutions for reducing overhead, enhancing engagement, and increasing profitability" through AI-powered catalog management.

## How they compare to us

BizNet AI is the closest to what Fishbone is building -- they're explicitly about making catalog data AI-ready, not just auditing it. But they appear to be extremely early stage.

**Overlap:**
- Catalog enrichment / transformation for AI agent discovery
- Structured data as the core value prop
- B2B, merchant-facing
- "Agentic commerce" framing

**Differences:**
- They seem more infrastructure/API-oriented ("semantic search engine", "PIM sync")
- We're leaning toward hands-on enrichment service, they seem more self-serve platform
- They're US-based (Delaware), we're Israel-based
- They appear to be very early -- 5 LinkedIn followers, 2-10 people, founded 2026

## Strengths

- Directly in the enrichment/transformation space, not just audit
- Platform approach (PIM sync, structured data ingestion) could scale
- "Agent-ready catalog" positioning is on point

## Weaknesses

- Extremely early -- barely any public presence
- Website doesn't render without JavaScript (bad sign for a product company)
- No visible customers, reviews, or case studies
- 5 LinkedIn followers suggests minimal traction
- No known funding
- Parent company (ConsumerGenie) appears to be a product review site -- unclear strategic fit

## Assessment

BizNet AI is directionally similar to Fishbone but doesn't appear to be a serious threat today. They're pre-traction with minimal market presence. Worth monitoring quarterly, but not an urgent competitive concern.
