## Overview

Enterprise knowledge graph and digital presence management platform. Originally known for local business listings management, Yext has repositioned around AI search and structured product data for the agentic commerce era. Advocates for "agent-ready product catalogs" — structured, machine-readable product data that AI agents can find, interpret, and act on. Publicly traded (NYSE: YEXT), ~1,400 employees.

**Company:** Yext, Inc.
**HQ:** New York, NY, USA
**Founded:** 2006
**Platform:** Platform-agnostic, integrates with Google, Bing, Apple Maps, and 200+ AI/search platforms
**Stage:** Public (NYSE: YEXT); ~$350M ARR

## Pricing

Enterprise contracts, typically $500/location/year and up for listings management. AI-specific tiers not publicly broken out.

## What they do

- Knowledge graph for structured product and business data — central source of truth across AI platforms
- AI Content Generation for product descriptions, social, and search
- "Agent-ready" product catalog structuring — cleaning, normalizing, enriching product data for LLM consumption
- AI search analytics (Yext Research — 2B+ data points on AI search trends)
- Google Product Catalog sync — product data to Google Knowledge cards
- Generative Answers for Search (April 2025) — AI-generated answer experiences
- Monitoring and optimization across 200+ platforms including ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini

## Strengths

- Massive enterprise customer base and distribution — trusted incumbent
- Knowledge graph architecture is genuinely strong infrastructure for structured product data
- Already integrated with 200+ AI and search platforms — distribution moat
- Publicly traded with significant resources for R&D
- Positioned early on "agent-ready catalog" narrative — thought leadership weight
- Not going anywhere — established enterprise contracts and recurring revenue

## Weaknesses

- Not primarily a catalog enrichment company — core is listings/knowledge management, AI catalog is an expansion
- Enterprise-only pricing and sales cycle — inaccessible to mid-market without significant investment
- Moving slowly relative to startups (quarterly releases vs. weekly shipping)
- Repositioning from local listings to AI commerce is a long journey; brand perception lags
- Not agentic-first — built on web era assumptions, adapting to AI era

## Threat level to Fishbone

**Adjacent** — Yext is advocating for and enabling "agent-ready catalogs" but through their knowledge graph platform, not purpose-built catalog enrichment. Their enterprise positioning and distribution are formidable, but they're not Fishbone's primary competition. Mid-market brands who can't afford Yext are exactly Fishbone's target. Long-term, if Yext ships a dedicated catalog enrichment product, they become a more significant threat.
