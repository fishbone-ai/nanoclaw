## Overview

AI-powered product content enrichment platform. Explicitly positions itself as "Product Content Optimization For Humans & Machines" — covering paid ads, organic search, AEO, and GEO. Generates and enriches product attributes, descriptions, metadata, and schema markup to improve visibility across traditional search and AI discovery platforms.

This is the closest direct competitor to Fishbone in market today.

**HQ:** Palo Alto, CA
**Stage:** Growth — multiple enterprise retail customers
**Funding:** Series B (raised $12M Series B in 2022; total ~$20M+)
**Target:** Mid-to-large retailers and brands

## What they do

Lily operates across three stacked layers:

### Data Layer
- Proprietary dataset: blend of retailer, public, and proprietary data sources
- AI models trained specifically on retail/commerce product content
- Measurement and analytics that feed back into model refinement

### Enrichment Layer
- **Attributes** — rich, nuanced attributes beyond basic color/cut, aligned with how customers actually search
- **Trends** — micro and macro trend alignment (what's hot now vs. what's enduring)
- **Occasions** — catalog alignment to real-world moments (holiday parties, birthday gifts, etc.)
- **Styles** — enduring style taxonomy (home, wardrobe, beauty)
- **Product copy** — descriptions and highlights for both shoppers and AI systems

### Application Layer (use cases)
- **Site search** — enriched attributes and synonyms boost on-site discovery
- **Paid ads** — data structured for Google Merchant Center, informed by consumer search intent
- **SEO & GEO** — structured data for visibility in traditional and AI search (ChatGPT, Gemini, etc.)
- **Marketplaces** — normalize and enrich data across third-party sellers

## How they compare to us

Lily AI is the most direct overlap with Fishbone's planned product. They already do what Fishbone is building — enrichment at the catalog level, AI-ready content generation, multi-channel optimization.

**Key differences:**
- Lily is established, enterprise-focused — we can target mid-market they may not serve well
- Lily's proprietary dataset is a real moat — they've been building it for years (founded ~2018-2019)
- Their AI is trained on fashion/retail specifically — strong in apparel, beauty, home
- No clear Shopify app or self-serve entry point — complex onboarding, enterprise sales motion
- Platform-agnostic — works with any commerce stack, not tied to one platform

**Where they're stronger:**
- Trend alignment feature is unique and defensible
- Occasion/lifestyle tagging is sophisticated
- Established enterprise customer base and sales machine
- Proprietary training data is years ahead of us

**Where we have opportunity:**
- Mid-market merchants priced out of Lily's enterprise motion
- Simpler / faster onboarding for smaller catalogs
- Agentic commerce framing (Lily still talks mostly SEO/GEO, not "AI agent discovery" specifically)
- Potential to be faster and more responsive to the rapidly evolving AEO landscape

## Strengths

- Most complete enrichment product in market — attributes, trends, occasions, copy, ads, SEO, GEO
- Proprietary training data is a real competitive moat
- Enterprise credibility and established sales machine
- "For Humans & Machines" positioning is on point
- Measurement layer creates a feedback loop (rare in this space)

## Weaknesses

- Enterprise sales motion likely means long cycles and high ACV — misses mid-market
- No visible self-serve or Shopify app entry point
- Website blocks direct traffic (lily.ai 403s) — could indicate CDN misconfiguration or geo-blocking issues
- Trend/occasion features are fashion-forward — may not apply well to other verticals
- Less visible in the "agentic commerce" conversation vs. SEO/GEO framing

## Assessment

Lily AI is the benchmark competitor. They're building what Fishbone wants to build, have a head start, and have raised real money. The good news: they're enterprise-first, likely expensive, and focused on specific verticals (fashion, beauty, home). Fishbone can differentiate on speed, mid-market accessibility, and explicit AEO/agent-readiness framing.

Long term, if Fishbone gets traction, Lily is the company most likely to become a meaningful competitor — or acquisition target/acquirer.
